NASA Techrise Payload

Instrumentation & Automation II | Sewanhaka High School


Completed:

June, 2022

Timeline:

6 Months (~8 hrs/week)

Role:

Instrumentation Design, Sensor Programmer, Test Simulation

As one of 57 winners of NASA Techrise's inaugural competition, I worked with my high school engineering class to design and fabricate an instrumentation payload that collected data at the edge of space in a hot air balloon. The payload, Atmospheric Collection of Noxious Gases, aimed to combat issues of climate and pollution with high-tech data collection methods.



Design Requirements

Our experiment had to align with numerous NASA guidelines in order to ensure proper performance in the harsh conditions of outer space, including the following:

  • Withstand temperatures as low as -40 degrees F
  • Perform consistently in a 0 pressure environment
  • Fit into a 4"x4"x8" cubesat





    • Design and Build

    To test the air at the edge of space for pollution, our team designed an instrumentation payload for the hot air balloon, choosing to use an Arduino REV 3 for its small physical size and large memory. After planning our design in Onshape, our team constructed the payload structure using laser cut wood panels, attaching sensors to a custom circuit board seperate from the Arduino.



    Programming

    I worked with a team to obtain PM2.5 readings from the PMSA003I sensor, as well as obtain altitude and temperature data from the DPS310 and BNO055.     Final Product

    Our payload launched on July 21st, 2023. It sucessfully collected 4 hours of data over the Midwest United States during a time when the California wildfires were raging strong. We shared this data with NASA and various other researchers to contribute to climate research
