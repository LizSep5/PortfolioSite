NASA Techrise Payload

Instrumentation & Automation II | Sewanhaka High School


Completed:

June, 2022

Timeline:

6 Months (~8 hrs/week)

Role:

Instrumentation Design, Sensor Programmer, Test Simulation

As one of 57 winners of NASA Techrise's inaugural competition, I worked with my high school engineering class to design and fabricate an instrumentation payload that collected data at the edge of space in a hot air balloon. The payload, Atmospheric Collection of Noxious Gases, aimed to combat issues of climate and pollution with high-tech data collection methods.



Design Requirements

Our experiment had to align with numerous NASA guidelines in order to ensure proper performance in the harsh conditions of outer space. Including the following:

  • Able to withstand temperatures as low as -40 degrees F
  • Withstand pressure as low as
  • Fit into a 6"x6"x8" cubesat



    • Ideation

    Our Ideation process began with brainstorming ~100 ideas and each team member choosing their top two to flesh out in a sketch. From there, we each prototyped a low-fidelity model of our favorite. We analyzed these three ideas through a Feasibility vs. Attractivness ranking, eventually combining aspects from each design to land at the most attractive product for our timeline, "Gloria."


    Process

    As the CAD lead, I modeled our final design in Fusion360 so we could visualize the parts we would need to assemble our guitar.

    We created our high-fidelity prototype out of the provided cutting board, utulizing tools like the bandsaw and scroll saw to cut the body and details. I soldered the electronics and we conducted several tests before assembly to ensure our pickup produced a sonsistently clean sound.

    Final Product

    The final product includes several design features which address our original design requirements:

  • Sound: f-holes and the semi-hollow body produces a warm sound while limiting feedback. 1 tone and 1 volume knob allow the user to further control sound quality.
  • Comfort: the semi-hollow body makes the guitar lightweight. We also made the guitar body slim, so the player can easily strum.
  • Style: f-holes double as a classic jazz style feature, as well as the oiled, wooden body.
  • Affordability: through our BOM calculations, the guitar costs less than $200 to produce, beating out our market comparisons.
