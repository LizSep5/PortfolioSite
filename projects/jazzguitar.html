Jazz Guitar

ENGN 0032 | Brown University


Completed:

December, 2024

Timeline:

1 Month (~40 hrs)

Role:

Design, CAD, Electronics

In pursuit of quality sound and style, our team created "Gloria," a jazz guitar that looks as good as it sounds, and won't break the bank.




Design Persona/Requirements

Our persona, Gloria, is an intermediate jazz guitar player who is looking for a guitar with consistent sound quality. After a competitive analysis of the current market, our team decided to innovate upon the classic jazz guitar model by providing similar sound quality and tone for a more affordable price. We chose the following design requirements:

  • Sound: Warm and resonant
  • Feel: Comfortable
  • Look: Classic and stylish
  • Price Point: Affordable




    • Ideation

    Our Ideation process began with brainstorming ~100 ideas and each team member choosing their top two to flesh out in a sketch. From there, we each prototyped a low-fidelity model of our favorite. We analyzed these three ideas through a Feasibility vs. Attractivness ranking, eventually combining aspects from each design to land at the most attractive product for our timeline, "Gloria."



    Process

    As the CAD lead, I modeled our final design in Fusion360 so we could visualize the parts we would need to assemble our guitar.

    We created our high-fidelity prototype out of the provided cutting board, utilizing tools like the bandsaw and scroll saw to cut the body and details. I soldered the electronics and we conducted several tests before assembly to ensure our pickup produced a sonsistently clean sound.



    Final Product


    The final product includes several design features which address our original design requirements:
  • Sound: f-holes and the semi-hollow body produces a warm sound while limiting feedback. 1 tone and 1 volume knob allow the user to further control sound quality.
  • Comfort: the semi-hollow body makes the guitar lightweight. We also made the guitar body slim, so the player can easily strum.
  • Style: f-holes double as a classic jazz style feature, as well as the oiled, wooden body.
  • Affordability: through our BOM calculations, the guitar costs less than $200 to produce, beating out our market comparisons.
