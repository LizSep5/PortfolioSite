The Morton Modular

Personal Project


In Progress:

June 2024 - Present

Role:

User Research, Design, CAD, Upholstery

In order to address the leisure needs of studio users at Stevens Institute of Tech., I am in collaboration with Jonathan Ramesh in making the Morton Modular, a set of two chairs that turn into a bench/bed.


Design Persona/Requirements

Our intended user is a member of the Morton Visual Arts and Tech (VART) studio. We picked the following values/ideas to represent this space and persona: community, modularity, academic, sleep, and simplicity.

  • Community
  • Modularity
  • Academic
  • Sleep
  • Simplicity



    • Ideation

    We conducted collaborative research and brainstorming on a Miro board, exploring current furniture practices that align with our chosen values. From there, we sketched and prototyped our own ideas in cardboard and rudimentary CAD,which we downselected through an iterative process. We landed on the Morton Modular because of its simple interlocking design and unique approach to community furniture.


    Process

    Together, Jonathan and I completed a CAD model of the Morton Modular design in Onshape, which helped us construct a 1/3 scale model. We revised several measurements after this test.

    Due to distance, we are still in the process of fabrication. We have fabricated the body and upholstery, but have yet to test the two together and finish the wood structure.
